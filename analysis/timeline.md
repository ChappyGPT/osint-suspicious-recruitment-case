# Investigation Timeline  

---

## Day 1 – Initial Contact & Suspicion  

The investigation began after identifying a **job offer targeting young women**, presenting:  

- “No experience required”  
- “High income”  
- Flexible working conditions  

After initial contact, the conversation quickly shifted toward **questions about sexual service limits**, which did not align with the original job description.  

### Insight  

At this stage, the offer already showed signs of **misrepresentation**, suggesting that the recruitment process might be intentionally misleading.  

---

## Day 2 – Identification of the Main Contact  

The phone number **653034010** was identified as the primary communication point.  

This number appeared consistently across:  

- Job advertisements  
- Social media profiles  
- External listings  

### Actions  

- Google searches of the phone number  
- Cross-checking across platforms  
- Initial correlation of results  

### Insight  

The phone number quickly became the **central pivot of the investigation**, linking multiple sources together.  

---

## Day 3 – Direct Communication Analysis  

Message exchanges associated with the number were analyzed.  

### Observations  

- Communication occurred exclusively via **WhatsApp**  
- Conversations included:  
  - Payment-related discussions  
  - Delays and repeated excuses  
  - Informal working conditions  

### Key Elements Identified  

- References to unpaid work  
- Repeated postponement of payments  
- Avoidance when confronted  

### Insight  

This provided **direct behavioral evidence**, reinforcing patterns later observed in external testimonies.  

---

## Day 4 – Domain Identification & Analysis  

The domain **miramedesdecasa.com** was identified through contact profiles and listings.  

### Actions  

- DNS queries (`dig`)  
- OSINT enumeration (`theHarvester`, SpiderFoot)  

### Findings  

- Domain status: **NXDOMAIN**  
- No active services  
- No associated infrastructure  

### Insight  

The absence of infrastructure suggested that the operation does not rely on **persistent technical assets**, but on external platforms.  

---

## Day 5 – Cross-Platform Correlation  

The phone number and domain were searched across multiple platforms:  

- Twitter/X  
- Facebook  
- Forums (Spalumi)  
- Google search results  

### Findings  

- Repeated use of the same phone number  
- Consistent job offer structure  
- Similar wording across different posts  

### Insight  

The activity showed a **distributed presence**, but with clear coordination through shared identifiers.

---

## Day 8 – OSINT Tool Analysis  

Several tools were used to expand the investigation:  

- SpiderFoot  
- theHarvester  
- Holehe  
- Maigret  

### Results  

- No strong identity correlations  
- No reusable usernames  
- Minimal infrastructure data  

### Insight  

The lack of results suggests **intentional fragmentation of identity and low digital persistence**.  

---

## Day 9 – Infrastructure Correlation  

An IP address was identified during analysis:  

- **199.34.228.159**  

### Findings  

- Associated with shared hosting  
- Multiple unrelated domains hosted on the same infrastructure  

### Insight  

The use of shared infrastructure further supports the idea of **low traceability and limited attribution**.  

---

## Day 10 – Pattern Consolidation  

All collected data was analyzed together.  

### Identified Operational Flow  

1. Job advertisement published  
2. Contact redirected to WhatsApp  
3. Conversation escalates  
4. Multiple identities used  
5. Phone numbers rotated over time  

### Insight  

The activity follows a **structured but low-tech model**, focused on adaptability rather than persistence.  

---

## Final Assessment  

Across all stages of the investigation, several consistent elements were identified:  

- Phone numbers as central nodes  
- Absence of persistent infrastructure  
- Lack of stable digital identity  
- Cross-platform presence  
- Evidence of evasive behavior  

### Final Insight  

> The operation is designed to minimize traceability by avoiding persistent digital assets.  

It relies on:  

- Communication channels (phone numbers)  
- Third-party platforms  
- Fragmented and replaceable identities  

---

## Closing Note  

This investigation shows that even in environments with:  

- No active domains  
- No infrastructure  
- No clear identity  

It is still possible to reconstruct an operational model through:  

- Pattern recognition  
- Cross-source correlation  
- Structured OSINT methodology  

The key was not finding a single identifier, but **connecting multiple weak signals into a coherent picture**.
